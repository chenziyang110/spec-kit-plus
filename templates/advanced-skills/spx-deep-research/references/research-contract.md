# Research contract

Research only questions that can change feasibility, architecture, dependency
choice, security, operations, rollout, or verification. For each question,
record the decision it controls and the evidence threshold for stopping.

Prefer authoritative documentation, standards, release notes, source code, and
reproducible runtime evidence. Separate quoted or observed facts from inference.
Resolve disagreement by source authority, version applicability, and a focused
experiment; do not average conflicting claims.

A spike proves only its tested environment and path. Capture prerequisites,
exact commands, output, cleanup, and gaps. The final planning handoff must state
what is safe to rely on, what remains conditional, and how implementation will
verify the claim at a real entry point.
